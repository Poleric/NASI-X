Solar System

Our solar system have 10 planet, that is Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune, Kristane, and Pologane.

Mercury

Composed mainly of rock and metal, is around 4.6 billion years old, has an orbital speed of 47.87 km/s, a surface temperature that varies from extremely hot to extremely cold, a mass of about 0.055 Earth masses, a small size with a diameter of about 4,880 kilometers, an average distance from Earth of about 77 million kilometers, a relatively high density, and a nearly spherical shape.

Venus

A rocky planet, approximately 4.6 billion years old, with an orbital speed of about 35.02 km/s, a scorching surface temperature due to its thick atmosphere, a mass roughly 0.815 times that of Earth, a similar size with a diameter of about 12,104 kilometers, an average distance from Earth of approximately 261 million kilometers, a density close to Earth's, and an almost spherical shape.

Earth

Primarily composed of rock and metal, is about 4.5 billion years old, has an orbital speed of about 29.78 km/s, a moderate average surface temperature, a mass of about 1 Earth mass, a size with a diameter of roughly 12,742 kilometers, a fixed average distance from itself, a density close to 5.52 g/cmÂ³, and an almost spherical shape.

Mars

Predominantly rocky, is approximately 4.6 billion years old, has an orbital speed of about 24.08 km/s, a surface temperature that can vary greatly, a mass around 0.107 Earth masses, a smaller size with a diameter of about 6,779 kilometers, an average distance from Earth ranging from 78 million to 401 million kilometers, a relatively low density, and a roughly spherical shape.

Jupiter

Consists mainly of gas and liquid, is roughly 4.5 billion years old, has an orbital speed of about 13.07 km/s, no well-defined surface temperature due to its gaseous nature, an immensely large mass approximately 318 times that of Earth, a colossal size with a diameter of about 139,822 kilometers, an average distance from Earth ranging from 588 million to 968 million kilometers, a relatively low density, and an oblate spheroid shape.

Saturn

Primarily composed of gas and liquid, is about 4.5 billion years old, has an orbital speed of about 9.68 km/s, no distinct surface temperature due to its gaseous composition, a massive body weighing about 95 times Earth's mass, a gigantic size with a diameter of roughly 116,464 kilometers, an average distance from Earth ranging from 1.2 billion to 1.7 billion kilometers, a relatively low density, and an oblate spheroid shape.

Uranus

Primarily composed of gas and ice, is around 4.5 billion years old, has an orbital speed of about 6.8 km/s, a frigid surface temperature due to its distance from the Sun, a mass of about 14.5 times that of Earth, a moderate size with a diameter of approximately 50,724 kilometers, an average distance from Earth varying from 2.6 billion to 2.9 billion kilometers, a relatively low density, and a slightly flattened spheroid shape.

Neptune

Primarily composed of gas and ice, is around 4.5 billion years old, has an orbital speed of about 5.43 km/s, a freezing surface temperature due to its distance from the Sun, a mass about 17 times that of Earth, a moderate size with a diameter of roughly 49,244 kilometers, an average distance from Earth fluctuating from 4.3 billion to 4.7 billion kilometers, a relatively low density, and a slightly flattened spheroid shape.
